Plivo STT End-of-Turn Detection: Engineering Summary

Author: Nishant Sou (Roll No: 24112075)

Track: End-of-Turn Detection (STT)

1. Executive Overview

Voice AI turn-taking requires balancing minimal response latency against user interruptions. A naive silence-only baseline results in severe delays (~1600 ms). This project implements a causal, prosodic and linguistic feature extraction pipeline combined with a metric-driven cross-validation training loop to minimize response delay while keeping false interruptions under 5%.

3. Human vs. Coding Agent Division of Labor

  • Human Direction: Identified structural flaws in proxy optimization (maximizing generic AUC instead of Plivo's partial-AUC delay/cutoff metric), diagnosed the Hindi threshold compression bug, established the core causal constraints, thought of implementing grid search to get the optimized numbers.
  • Coding Agent Assistance: Rapidly prototyped and implemented my strategies as i explained, handled repetitive code refactoring across training iterations.

4. Why This Beats the Status Quo

Unlike raw silence thresholds or uncalibrated models that trigger premature interruptions or excessive wait times, our final Random Forest architecture utilizes linguistic turn-yielding bundles (creaky voice, relative energy floor distance, and pitch percentiles) paired with cost-sensitive training weights that heavily penalize short-hold errors. This enables robust generalization across multilingual datasets (English and Hindi) well below the 5% cutoff budget.
